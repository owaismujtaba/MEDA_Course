## Executive Summary
This report provides a comprehensive analysis of India's geoeconomic fragmentation and "derisking" progress. By integrating chapter-level data with total trade flows across all major partners, we identify structural vulnerabilities and shifts in India's trade architecture.

**Key Findings:**
1.  **Macro-Level Risk:** India's trade deficit with China has reached an all-time high of **$112 Billion** in 2024, driven by a surge in imports (+115% since 2020) while exports remain stagnant.
2.  **Strategic Divergence:** Successful "derisking" in critical sectors like **Fertilizers** is overshadowed by deepening dependence in **Machinery and Electronics**.
3.  **Partner Shifting:** Russia has emerged as a major trade partner in 2024, displacing traditional partners in the top import list, though primarily in the energy sector.

---

## 1. Trade Balance and the China Deficit
The "India-China Trade Balance" plot reveals a widening gap that poses a significant macroeconomic challenge.

- **Import Surge:** Imports from China rose from **$58.8B in 2020** to **$126.9B in 2024**.
- **Stagnant Exports:** Exports to China have declined from a peak of $23B in 2021 to **$14.9B in 2024**.
- **Deficit Explosion:** The trade deficit has nearly tripled in 4 years, highlighting that "Derisking" from a value perspective is not yet materializing.

---

## 2. Partner Diversification Trends
Using the **All Countries** dataset, we analyzed the share of India's top 10 partners.

- **China's Dominance:** China remains the single largest source of imports, significantly ahead of the next partners.
- **The Russian Surge:** Russia (RUS) moved to the #2 spot in 2024 ($67B), driven by crude oil diversification.
- **Traditional Partners:** Shares of the USA and UAE remain stable, but have not significantly displaced China's share in the manufacturing-heavy categories.

---

## 3. Sectoral "Derisking" (ASCA Analysis)
Using ANOVA-Simultaneous Component Analysis (ASCA), we isolated the "Partner Effect" to see which chapters are structural drivers of China's dominance.

#### A. The "Atmanirbhar" Successes
- **Fertilizers (Chapter 31):** Dependence dropped from **47.33% to 11.08%** (2015-2024).
- **Iron and Steel (Chapter 72):** Dependence decreased to 17.19%.

#### B. The Increasing Vulnerabilities
- **Machinery (Chapter 84):** China's share rose to **44.89%**.
- **Electronics (Chapter 85):** Remains the highest risk at **57.00%**.
- **Plastics (Chapter 39):** Share doubled to **29.22%**.

## 5. Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Focus on Value-Added Exports:** The stagnant export growth ($14.9B) suggests India is failing to penetrate Chinese markets even as it consumes more Chinese goods.
2.  **Energy vs. Manufacturing:** While the "Russian Surge" helps diversify energy imports, it does not address the manufacturing dependence on China (Chapters 84/85).
3.  **Targeted Import Substitution:** ASCA loadings suggest that Plastics (Ch 39) and Machinery (Ch 84) should be the next priority for the PLI (Production Linked Incentive) scheme.
